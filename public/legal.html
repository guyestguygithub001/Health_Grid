2. Privacy Policy & Data Protection Notice

Compliant with NDPA 2023, GDPR, & HIPAA

The GHG is committed to protecting patient privacy, protected health information (PHI), and personal data across all healthcare facilities in Plateau State.

2.1 Categories of Data Collected

  • Personal & Contact Data: Name, phone number, National Identification Number (NIN), date of birth, LGA, community address.
  • Protected Health Information (PHI): Clinical notes, SOAP encounters, ICD-11 diagnostic codes, lab results, prescriptions, vitals, allergies.
  • Biometric Data: Local encrypted fingerprint/face hashes stored strictly in on-device SQLite databases for offline authentication.
  • Technical & Usage Data: Anonymized audit logs, IP address, device telemetry, vector clock sync metadata.

2.2 Purpose & Legal Basis for Processing

Data processing is conducted under lawful bases established by the Nigeria Data Protection Act (NDPA 2023) and GDPR Article 6/9: Medical Diagnosis and Public Health Management, Execution of Healthcare Service Contracts, and Vital Interests of Patients.

2.3 Local Data Sovereignty & Storage

All patient records remain within sovereign data storage boundaries. Biometric templates are stored locally in hardware-encrypted caches and never transmitted across unencrypted public channels.

2.4 Patient Data Rights

Patients have the right to: Request a full copy of their health records (FHIR / JSON export), request rectification of erroneous demographics, object to non-essential processing, and lodge complaints with the Nigeria Data Protection Commission (NDPC).

3. Clinical Decision Support & Medical Disclaimer

FDA 2022 Cures Act Section 520 & NAFDAC CDS Guidelines
🩺

CLINICAL DECISION SUPPORT NOTICE

The Global Health Grid provides clinical information, ICD-11 coding aids, drug interaction alerts, and AI triage suggestions for informational and decision-support purposes only. It does not independently make primary clinical diagnoses or prescribe treatments.

1. No Doctor-Patient Relationship: Interaction with the GHG system or Patient Portal does not create a formal physician-patient relationship until a clinical encounter is initiated with a licensed practitioner.

2. Professional Judgment Required: Attending healthcare professionals retain ultimate responsibility for evaluating patients, verifying system alerts, prescribing medications, and rendering medical care.

3. Use at Own Risk: Information presented by automated algorithms is based on populated patient parameters and should be independently validated prior to clinical administration.

6. Data Breach & Incident Response Protocol

72-Hour Regulatory / 60-Day Patient SLA

In accordance with the Health Breach Notification Rule and NDPA 2023 Guidelines, GHG maintains an active Security Incident Response SLA:

  1. Immediate Containment (T+0 to T+4h): Automated isolation of compromised vector nodes and credential revocation.
  2. Regulatory Notification (T+72h): Formal notification submitted to the NDPC and relevant supervisory authorities.
  3. Patient & Facility Notice (T+60d): Written disclosure provided to affected individuals detailing nature of breach and mitigation steps.
